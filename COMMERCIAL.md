## When you need a commercial license

You need a commercial license if you, your company, or your project does any of the following:

- Operate as a for-profit entity and use Memory Fort in furtherance of business activities (including internal tooling at a for-profit company)
- Sell Memory Fort, modified versions, or services built around it
- Embed Memory Fort in a commercial product
- Host Memory Fort as a paid service for customers
- Use Memory Fort to deliver consulting services for fee

**"Your company"** as defined in the license includes any legal entity, sole proprietorship, or organization you work for, plus all organizations that have control over, are under the control of, or are under common control with that organization.

## When you do NOT need a commercial license

Memory Fort is free, with no separate license required, for:

- Personal use, hobby projects, evaluation, and learning
- Academic research and education at non-profit institutions
- Use by charitable organizations, government institutions, public research organizations, public safety/health organizations, environmental protection organizations
- Open-source projects under non-commercial-compatible licenses
- Trying Memory Fort out before deciding whether to license it commercially

## Discussion-stage use

If you are evaluating Memory Fort to decide whether to license it commercially, that evaluation itself is permitted under the non-commercial license. You do not need a commercial license to try it out, run a proof-of-concept, or discuss internally whether it fits. The commercial license is required before you put Memory Fort into production for business purposes.
